COMM 2034: Visual Media

Professor

The professor for my Visual Media class is Dr. Carlos Evia. Dr. Evia is a fun loving teacher who is very easy to learn from. He is also grading this project, so is that why I am saying nice things about him? You will never know.

Education

Dr. Evia earned his MSC from Universidad LaSalle in Mexico City. He then went on to complete his PhD at Texas Tech University.

Interests

Outside of teaching Visual Media, Dr. Evia has expertise in many other subjects. Namely, he is expereienced in Technical Communication, User Documentation, Structured Authoring, Workplace Safety, and Hispanic Construction Workers. He is also a member ofthe Darwin Information Typing Architecture Technical Committee and the Society for Technical Communication, among other things. He was awarded the Outstanding Faculty Outreach award by the Virginia Tech College of Liberal Arts and Human Sciences in May of 2011.

Research

Like many Virginia Tech professors, Dr. Evia participates in University sponsored research. His most significant research opportunity came when he named a Co-Principal Investigator for the "Microprocesses of Latino Construction Workers' Health: Feasability of CATS" project. Dr. Evia was awarded $250,000 for the reseach, and he worked with the National Institute of Occupational Safety and Health from 2009-2012 on the project.

Class Description

In Visual Media, we learn the essential skills to communicate through other mediums besides just text. Our four main projects involved employing communication techniques through page layout, photos, videos, and web presence.

Opinion

I honestly have enjoyed Visual Media Class. At first, I was very nervous, because I do not have very much expereince when it comes to video and photo editing. However, the class has been able to challenge me without being so hard that I feel discouraged. I have learned a lot of essential skills for my desired future jobs, namely how to use Adobe Photoshop and Premiere.

Expected Grade

I currently have a B plus in this class. We still have a couple of projects to go, so if I do well on those, I should be able to bring it up to an A or A minus.
